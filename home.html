Gardens are important to the planet because, despite being human-made, they represent a natural environment. Well-established community gardens may help secure food supplies and provide essential infrastructural support following a disaster. However, first and foremost, community gardens help strengthen social interactions, relieve stress and build the social capital that is needed when a disaster strikes.
